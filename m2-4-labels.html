"标签是判断"听起来抽象。来看一个真实事件——AI 把"哪些艺术品该被记住"这种事，翻车了。

"Labels are judgments" sounds abstract. Real-world case: AI botched "which artworks deserve to be remembered."

📍 2020 年代2020s

AI 文物识别系统：低估非西方文化遗产

AI Heritage Recognition: Undervaluing Non-Western Art

多家科技公司开发的"全球艺术品 AI 识别"系统被发现：识别欧洲油画准确率 92%，识别非洲面具、亚洲青铜器、原住民艺术只有 60%。

Global art recognition AIs from major tech companies were found to: identify European oil paintings at 92% accuracy, but African masks, Asian bronzes, and indigenous art at just 60%.

原因（很尴尬）Cause (embarrassing)

训练数据 78% 来自欧美博物馆。"什么是艺术"这个标签——已经被打上了文化偏见。AI 不是种族主义，但它学了一份种族主义的标签清单。

78% of training data came from Western museums. "What counts as art" — the label itself carried cultural bias. AI isn't racist, but it learned a label set that is.

问题：怎么修这个问题？Q: How would you fix this?
✓ 最佳答案：CBest answer: C

只加数据（A）没用——如果新数据还是欧美学者标的，偏见还在。

Just adding data (A) fails — if the new data is still labeled by Western experts, bias persists.

只让其他文化的人参与（B）也不够——他们可能只能影响小部分数据。

Just inviting others (B) isn't enough — they may only touch a fraction of data.

真正的修复需要：① 更多元的数据 ② 标签者来自多元文化 ③ 上线后持续被审查——这才是负责任地解决标签偏见的标准做法。

Real fix needs: ① diverse data ② diverse labelers ③ ongoing audit post-launch — the responsible standard.

📚 延伸阅读Read more： 这种"数据标签里的隐形偏见"现在有个学术名："算法殖民主义"（Algorithmic Colonialism）——西方科技公司用西方标准定义全世界"什么是好的"。中国学者也越来越关注这个问题。 This "hidden bias in data labeling" is now called "Algorithmic Colonialism" — when Western tech firms use Western standards to define what's "good" globally. Chinese scholars are paying increasing attention.
